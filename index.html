OUR MISSION

Our Mission in life is to provide a safe and a well-knit home for orphans and abandoned female children. We stay connected and linked with these children for close moments with them. They are often forced to forego basic necessities in view of financial constraints from their family and the mission of RUVA Foundation is to offer its best ability for fund sourcing.

What is Ruva Foundation?

RUVA Foundation is a charitable trust founded by Mr. A. Saravan Stanley, an ordinary personality, with an intention of helping EWS (Economically Weaker Section) of the Society and the poor people in fulfilment of their aims and objectives as listed below:

ALCOHOLISM

Alcoholism is rampant in the families of the downtrodden children and this has been affecting them in several ways.

MEDICAL ASSISSTANCE

Arranging medical relief and immediate assistance to the poor when they meet with fatal accidents and people affected by diseases.

FINANCIAL ASSISSTANCE

Extending financial help to the poor and needy to meet their educational expenses especially the orphans and the neglected ones.

AWARENESS

Creating general awareness among the poor people and the slum dwellers about the environmental hazardous like drugs and alcohol addiction, etc.

OUR VISION

  • Providing and rendering Group Counselling which is considered necessary for broken families.
  • Knowledge sharing, creative skills capability, to enable foresee a ‘Better Tomorrow’ and to stand on their own feet.
  • Motivating children physically and mentally fit for any situations, to be non-aggressive, avoid barsh attitude so as to face a balanced life.

IMPACT

NGO was established in 2003

3000

CHILDREN

3000 boys and girls from slum area benefited in furthering their education, through tuition centres, from the year 2003 to 2016. The downtrodden children from the age of 13 years onwards, were from RUVA Charitable Trust, Koramangala and “Comfort Home for Little Angels” Hostel, located at Dommasandra, who received this privilege from RUVA Foundation Charitable Trust. The following supports were rendered for the slum children: Fees Books, School Bags, Shoes, Uniform, Sweaters.

800

FAMILIES

“Parents Awareness Program” are being conducted twice yearly, by the Managing Trustee Director himself, both at Koramangala Centre and Comfort Home Hostel Centre. The family members participates in the Awareness Program and adhere to the advises as given by the Managing Trustee. The parents do benefit from this Awareness Program. The parents advice their children with regard to the behavioral attitude, systematic studies and self-cleanliness, to live as good citizens.

52

SCHOOLS

RUVA Foundation supported 52 schools, both Government Aided and Private Schools. Our tuition teachers and other officials interact with the school teachers and Principal, from time-to-time to get their feed-back on the progress of the children, in studies.

TESTIMONIALS

Maria Madhalena

With immense help of the Director of RUVA Foundation Charitable Trust, I was admitted at “Comfort Home for Little Angels” Hostel during 2011, at the age of 13 years. This was due to my family background and my inability to concentrate on my studies at home. My father expired in view of being a heavy boozer, after which my mother totally became mentally disturbed. I am staying at this Hostel for the past seven years. After my stay at “Comfort Home”, I did very well at my studies and secured a first class in 2ndPUC. The Officials at “Comfort Home”took pains in securing College admission for me and will now shortly move on forward to 2nd B.Com. Without the sincere help from “Comfort Home” authorities, I could not have dreamt that I will pursue a College Education as well as excel in academics. The Trustees / generous donors assisted me with payment of College Fees, Uniform as well as books. I find no words to thank the Director of RUVA Charitable Trust as well as other Officials who assisted me in different aspects. Education is the key to my future success in life. I have no end to my Education and hence, will aspire to reach heights.

S.R. Lavanya

After my father expired in view of being an alcoholic, my mother left me in the lurch and had no support whatsoever from anybody. The public therefore, approached the Managing Trustee of RUVA Trust “Comfort Home for Little Angels Hostel” . I was admitted in 6th Std, in a Government School, when I was 11 years old. I found solace in this Hostel environment, ever since my life began with a bright star.
I am now in 10th Std and will be promoted to 1st PU shortly. I am proud to say that I would not have even stepped into a School, without the intervention of RUVA Trust.
“Comfort Home for Little Angels” Hostel, had changed my life and I am confident of looking up for a bright future ahead.

J.P. Angel

A brief note from the Mother of Baby Angel.
Having an alcoholic husband, I could not bring up my baby in the home atmosphere. Toying with my baby’s future, I had to seek a job for myself as a domestic help. I brought my ten months old baby to “Comfort Home for Little Angels” through the kind help of the Trustee Director. I left my baby under the care of the Hostel Warden.

7-year old Angel speaks..... I am now 7 years studying in a Government School in 3rd Std. The Warden took all personal pains to bring me up from a baby’s stage till now. It makes me very proud to feel that I am an inmate of “Comfort Home for Little Angels” from a baby. In view of me being the youngest of “Comfort Home”, the other elder girls pamper me and show immense love and affection and special attention.

Managing Trustee

Shravan Stanley

Mr. Saravan Stanley is not just an individual. He is of an exemplary personality who has been / and still devoting his life for the downtrodden children, who are in-housed at “Comfort Home for Little Angels” and the tuition centers of RUVA Foundation at Koramangala, for both drop-outs and those interested in continuation of furthering their education. He is a simple man of few words, but clear in his thoughts and works. He held the reins of RUVA Foundation, which was founded in the year 2003, for the upliftment of the less fortunate female children and endeavoured to provide a well-knit shelter with the best comfort zone facilities that could be afforded, through the generosity of kind hearted donors. All the puzzles of this shelter fell into its place and is now known as “Comfort Home for Little Angels” located at Dommasandra Circle, Varthur Road, Bangalore 562125, wherein only female children from the age of six to eighteen are in-housed.

Project Co-ordinator (Hostel-in-charge)

Mrs. G. Rukmani

Mrs. Rukmani is the senior most Pioneer attached to RUVA Foundation, since 2003. Her main intention was to work out a space for the downtrodden children and identified selection of young slum children left out at bay by their family and undertook a great work for the Society, from then till date. After grouping in the selected children, she brought to the notice of each child to learn more about having their own fundamental rights. During yester times, these children had little or no access to food or education and were exploited daily. She streamlined the situation of the children and brought out a ‘rights-based’ approach for entitlements available to all the children without discrimination.
